    How this was made

    200 Meters and Down — the book and its narration alike — was written by Claude Opus 4.8 (1M context), Anthropic’s large-context model, running in Claude Code. A verified accuracy canon — a pinned timeline, notation standard, and glossary anchored to thirteen original editions of the ARRL Radio Amateur’s Handbook (1927–1983) — governed every chapter-writing and technical-accuracy-auditing agent in the build, so the ten sections agree on every date, formula, and band edge.

    The narration is synthesized: eight neural voices — men and women in American, British, Australian, and Irish accents — each reading all ten sections, from the spark-gap era to software-defined radio. Start with the Introduction for the full story of how the book was built.
